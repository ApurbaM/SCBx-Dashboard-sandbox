Section 01

Why This Workshop, Why Now

SCBX is transforming into an AI-first enterprise by 2028. The sandbox program (March–May 2026) must deliver production-grade, beta-ready POCs that reimagine customer engagement from first principles — not layer AI onto existing workflows. With the June 2026 sandbox deadline and Q3/Q4 go-to-market, the window to align on the CX blueprint is now.

Workshop Objective

In one day, co-design an end-to-end CX blueprint that integrates Conversational Banking, Hyper-Personalization, and Autonomous Operations into two flagship customer journeys — with clear architecture, data requirements, success metrics, and a 90-day delivery roadmap — using McKinsey's LEAP blueprint methodology.

Scope: Two Integrated Journeys

J1
Personal Banker Mode (75% of POC)
Engagement-first, hyper-personalized day-in-the-life with Jarvis
  • START: Personalized morning brief — financial wellness, nudges, next-best-actions
  • DURING: Context-aware servicing — spend insights, portfolio, bill payments, smart offers
  • END: Service-to-sales transitions — credit card upgrade, DCA investment, PointX redemption
J2
Receptionist + Butler Mode (25% of POC)
Conversation-first seamless servicing with service-to-sales in every interaction
  • DURING: Tone, content personalization & in-call NBA for top 3 service intents
  • POST: Personalized next-best-actions (1–2 non-sales, 1 sales, 1 contextual to last conversation)
  • OFFLINE: Personalized video engagement for retention and habit formation
Section 02

The LEAP Blueprint Framework

McKinsey's LEAP methodology structures the day into four phases — each building on the last to move from insight to validated blueprint in a single sprint.

L
LOOK
Understand the customer. Map current-state pain points, data signals, and opportunity spaces across both journeys.
E
EXPLORE
Ideate the future. Design "10x better" AI-native experiences for each journey touchpoint, unconstrained by today.
A
ACT
Architect the blueprint. Map the service blueprint end-to-end: frontstage, backstage, AI agents, data flows, HITL handoffs.
P
PROVE
Define success and next steps. Set KPIs, prioritize build sprints, assign owners, and lock the 90-day delivery plan.
Section 03

Workshop Storyline

The day follows a dramatic arc — from grounding in reality, through creative tension, to a locked blueprint. Each phase has a clear "aha moment" that builds momentum.

1
8:30 — Opening

"The Moment of Truth"

Open with a real customer call recording or anonymized service complaint that exposes the gap between what SCB EASY delivers today and what customers expect. Set the emotional anchor: "This is why we're here." Share global AI-banking benchmarks — DBS, Nubank, Cleo — to establish the competitive urgency.

2
9:00 — LOOK phase

"Walking in Eva's Shoes"

Introduce Eva — a 31-year-old marketing manager, Wealth Potential segment. Walk through her actual day: morning commute, checking balances, paying bills, wondering about investments. Map her current journey on the wall — every friction point, every drop-off, every moment of confusion. The room sees the journey through the customer's eyes, not the bank's.

3
10:30 — EXPLORE phase

"What If Jarvis Knew You?"

Live demo of the Morning Briefing prototype. The room sees Eva's personalized morning brief, her financial wellness score, Jarvis paying her bills, nudging a card upgrade. The creative tension: "This is what's possible in 90 days. What else should Eva's ideal day look like?" Breakout groups ideate 10x experiences for each touchpoint.

4
1:00 — ACT phase

"Building the Machine Behind the Magic"

The hardest session. Teams architect the service blueprint: what the customer sees (frontstage), what the AI agents do (backstage), what data flows where, where humans step in. Map the 7-agent pipeline (Financial Health Scorer → Nudge Generator → Task Prioritizer → Insight Engine → Savings Advisor → Alert Engine → Conversational AI) against real SCB data systems.

5
3:30 — PROVE phase

"Making It Real"

Shift from vision to execution. Define the KPIs that prove this works: DAU increase, lead volume, x-sell conversion, call center migration, NPS. Prioritize the 5 sandbox use cases. Lock sprint assignments. Walk out with a signed blueprint and 90-day plan, not just ideas.

6
5:00 — Close

"The Commitment"

Each workstream lead presents their blueprint section to the Group CEO / sponsor. The room votes on the "north star" experience. Close with personal commitments: "What will you do in the next 48 hours to make this real?"

Section 05

Breakout & Exercise Design

Exercise 1: Current-State Journey Mapping (LOOK)

Wall Template

Pre-printed horizontal journey map with swim lanes: Customer Actions, Frontstage (App/Call/Branch), Backstage (Systems), Data/Signals, Emotions. Color-coded sticky notes for pain points (red), moments of truth (gold), and opportunities (blue).

Input Data

Pre-prepared packets: Top 10 call center intents with volumes, branch visit driver analysis, SCB EASY feature usage heatmap, NPS verbatims, current personalization capabilities (CCOM, Sonic, AI vertise).

Exercise 2: Future-State Ideation (EXPLORE)

Group A: START — Morning Brief

Design the ideal first 60 seconds of Eva's day on SCB EASY. What does the home screen show? What does the morning brief contain? How does it differ for 3 personas (Wealth Potential, Mass, SME)? What data powers each element?

Group B: DURING — In-Journey

Design how Jarvis handles: (1) a payment anomaly alert, (2) a loan servicing question, (3) a spend insight nudge. Map the conversational flow, AI agent handoffs, and context engine inputs for each.

Group C: END — Service-to-Sales

Design the seamless transition: Eva finishes paying a bill → Jarvis suggests a card upgrade. Map the triggers, offer logic, value optimizer, and channel orchestrator. How does this work for cross-PortCo (CardX credit card, SCB WEALTH fund, PointX redemption)?

Persona Cards

Pre-prepared persona cards for 3 customer archetypes: Eva (31, Marketing Manager, Wealth Potential), Somchai (45, Small Business Owner, SME), Nattapong (24, Fresh Graduate, Mass). Each card has income, products, behavioral signals, and life context.

Exercise 3: Use Case Blueprint Sprint (ACT)

Carousel Format

Five tables, each with a large pre-printed service blueprint template (Customer Actions → Frontstage → Line of Interaction → Backstage AI Agents → Line of Visibility → Support Systems → Data/APIs → Guardrails). Leaders rotate between tables every 15 minutes, ensuring cross-pollination. A "table captain" (McKinsey) stays at each table to maintain continuity. Each table produces a completed blueprint and a "top 3 blockers" list.

Section 09

Facilitation Principles

  • Customer-back, not tech-forward: Every discussion starts with "what does Eva need?" before "what can we build?"
  • Transformational, not incremental: Challenge every "can't do" with "what if we could?" — the mandate is reimagination from first principles
  • Cross-PortCo by default: Every use case must consider CardX, PointX, SCB WEALTH, and MONIX integration opportunities
  • Bias to tangibility: Use the working prototype as the anchor — critique what exists, extend what's missing
  • Decisions, not discussions: Every session ends with a locked decision or a named owner for resolution within 48 hours
  • 3 personas, not 1: Test every design against Eva (Wealth Potential), Somchai (SME), and Nattapong (Mass) to ensure scalability
